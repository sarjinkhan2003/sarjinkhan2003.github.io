Building Interpretable AI for Real-World Impact

I am a final-year Computer Science undergraduate at the American International University-Bangladesh (AIUB), maintaining a near-perfect CGPA of 3.98/4.00. My research lives at the intersection of Explainable AI, Human-Computer Interaction, and deep learning systems.

I believe that powerful AI is meaningless unless it is understandable. My work focuses on making complex machine learning models interpretable and accessible — from XAI-driven accident severity prediction to lightweight transformer architectures for resource-constrained environments.

As a co-author on 8 research works spanning IEEE conferences, IJECE, and multiple international venues, I bring rigorous academic training to every problem. I am passionate about Bangla NLP, bridging the digital divide for Bengali-speaking communities through tools like DocReader BD and the Bangla Lemmatizer.

Bangla Lemmatizer

A novel Bangla lemmatizer using an English-subsidiary cross-lingual approach to map inflected Bengali words to root forms. Addresses a critical gap in Bangla NLP tooling. Includes lexicon database, suffix-stripping rules, and web interface.
